The National Championship Season

The 1920 Everett Seagulls, affectionately known as "Baggy's Boys," are considered one of the greatest high school football teams in history. Led by legendary coach Enoch Bagshaw, the team finished 8-0-1 (excluding alumni games), outscoring opponents 412-23.

The season culminated in a "National Championship" game played on New Year's Day 1921 against East Technical High School of Cleveland, Ohio. Everett won 16-7 before a massive crowd at Athletic Field. The team featured a "flying circus" offense and a suffocating defense that shut out 6 of their 9 opponents, including college freshmen and military teams.

Star Players

The team was anchored by George Wilson, a halfback who would go on to become a College Football Hall of Famer at UW. Other key contributors included Captain Lester Sherman (Fullback), Glenn "Scoop" Carlson (QB), and Chalmer "Brute" Walters (Center).
